Government targets

From as early as the 1970s, the Government has introduced measures to promote a smoke-free lifestyle in Singapore. In the early days of Singapore's independence, smoking was allowed indoors but it was not long before the Smoking Act was introduced in October 1970 to prohibit smoking in omnibuses, cinemas and theatres. The country's smoking rate has shrunk from 18.3% in 1992 to 10.6% in 2019, through a combination of legislative and public education efforts. Authorities have regulated tobacco advertising and conducted multiple anti-smoking campaigns, mindful of its allure with the young. They have also raised the minimum legal age, made tobacco products more expensive with higher taxes, and prohibited smoking in even more places. Still, the Government wants to reduce the smoking rate even further, to below 10% of the population by this year. To further bring down the rate, a range of options are being considered by the Government. For instance, the Government could consider offering better protection from secondhand smoke, further increasing the tobacco tax and regulating tobacco flavours.

Smoking bans in Singapore

September 1970

Smoking was first banned in buses, cinemas and theaters.

November 1987

Smoking was banned in the Singapore MRT.

July 2005

The ban was extended to bus interchanges and shelters, public toilets and public swimming complexes.

July 2006

The ban was extended to coffee shops and hawker centres.

July 2007

The ban was extended to entertainment nightspots, including pubs, bars, lounges, dance clubs, and night clubs.

January 2009

The ban was extended to all children's playgrounds, exercise areas, markets, underground and multi-story car parks, ferry terminals and jetties. It was also extended to non-air-conditioned areas in offices, factories, shops and shopping complexes.

November 2010

Citizens of Singapore supported the Towards Tobacco-Free Singapore online campaign. The campaign promotes a proposal to prevent the supply of tobacco to Singaporeans born from the year 2000.

January 2013

The ban was extended to all common areas of a residential block, all pedestrian overhead bridges, 5m from the bus stops and hospital outdoor compounds.

June 2016

The ban was extended to reservoirs, as well as parks managed by JTC, town councils, and NParks. The ban also includes SAF and MHA camps, where smoking is already banned.

June 2017

Food & beverage outlets are no longer allowed new smoking corners.

October 2017

The ban was extended to autonomous universities' compounds, private hire vehicles, private education institutes, within 5m of all educational institutions, excursion buses and trishaws.

January 2019

Smoking was banned along the Orchard Road shopping district. Smokers can only light up within designated smoking areas in the precinct. Smoking corners in eateries within the precinct were also removed.
